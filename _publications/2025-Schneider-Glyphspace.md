The visual analysis of high-dimensional data requires innovative approaches to make complex feature structures explorable without substantial loss of information. This paper examines the current state of the art through a literature review, with a specific focus on the Glyphboard system. At its core is the synergy between dimensionality reduction (DR) and the enrichment of projections using glyphs. While DR reveals global data structures, glyphs enable the recovery of specific feature details directly within the 2D space. Glyphboard is evaluated as an integrative system that unites several theoretical concepts. However, the study identifies potential for optimization regarding performance and f lexibility, serving as a starting point for future developments in the field of visual analytics.
